WHAT IF... you could give someone a digital souvenir
of their day?

The Thinkery is the new Austin Children's Museum. While my time as an intern there was short, a lot of the fun designs I did were still floating around my head. I wanted to incorporate them into a digital project somehow, thus a concept for a Thinkery
app was born.

How do you get people to share your content?

Having worked on a marketing team for most of my career and played a role in social media coordination, I would hear this question over and over. The most obvious answer is that you create content your audience is genuinely interested in and useful to them. It's easier said than done. I designed a concept for a Thinkery app because I was pretty sure there could be another answer to that question. How do you get people to share your content?

You help them make their own great content.

PERSONA

WHAT IT DOES

I will be the first to say that this answer will not work for everybody. In fact, it will not work for almost anyone except experience-based places that need to gain loyal followers. It was still an idea I was eager to explore, so I first set about thinking about a typical Thinkery user. As you can see from the chart above, Sheila is a millenial mom who values the quality time she gets to spend with her kids outside of school. Because the lines at the Thinkery can get long during special events and weekend peak hours, the app allows Sheila to reserve tickets the day of her visit. It also gives her the option to order lunch while her kids are busy at an exhibit, saving her time in line at the museum's busy food trucks. The app is ambitious, which is why I've chosen to focus more on the most complicated aspect, the Souvenir.

FOCUS: TAKE, CREATE, SHARE, PRINT

As you can see from the persona above, Sheila is a millenial mom and works from her home in north Austin. She wants both a small physical token of her day with her kids AND a digital one she can send to friends and family. I started thinking about the ways she would need to get to the things she wanted through the app. What started out as a scrolling menu, soon turned into a permanent smaller one on the bottom of the screen. The most important development of the design was that a user had to have access to a starting point of any of the outcomes—TAKE, CREATE, SHARE, or PRINT—all within one touch.

After the previous design, a more conceptual model was needed to make sense of it all.


At this point, I began to work on the transitions from each section of the app. The first digital mockups appeared with the static menu.


THE RESULT

  • TAKE

    The user takes the picture or video, and it ends up in a queue in the bottom. Selecting one of the images lets the user edit it. This instance has the user changing the brightness, then saving.

  • CREATE I

    In the Create section, the user selects pictures or videos from a gallery. Selecting the arrow button creates a list of the items that are selected.

  • CREATE II

    The user is able to re-order their selections in the list view, then select the timeline view in order to add robots and mustaches to their heart's desire. Additionally, users would be able to add music to their creations.

  • SHARE

    Similar to the Create section, a user will select the pictures or videos from a gallery they want to share, then select each social network they want to share it on. A message option is available before the user presses send.

  • PRINT

    Users make their selections from a gallery, then are able to pick up their prints at the Thinkery's front desk.
